Lamar Jackson: Why the Ravens Should Consider Trading the Star QB

Lamar Demeatrice Jackson Jr., born on January 7, 1997, is an exceptional American football quarterback for the Baltimore Ravens of the National Football League (NFL). Known for his dual-threat capabilities, Jackson's journey from his humble beginnings to becoming a two-time NFL MVP has been nothing short of extraordinary.

Who is Lamar Jackson?

Lamar Jackson, hailing from Pompano Beach, Florida, was raised by his mother after the untimely passing of his father when he was just eight years old. His natural talent for football was evident from a young age, with reports of him throwing a football 20 yards at the mere age of eight. His remarkable journey from playing Pop Warner football to winning the Heisman Trophy in college, and ultimately becoming the face of the Baltimore Ravens, has been an inspiration to many.

The Rise of Lamar Jackson

After being drafted by the Ravens in 2018, Jackson's career skyrocketed when he took over as the starting quarterback, demonstrating his prowess as a dual-threat quarterback. In 2019, he made history by leading the season in touchdown passes and setting a single-season rushing record for his position. His outstanding performance earned him the prestigious NFL MVP title, making him the second unanimous MVP in league history. Jackson's ability to consistently deliver on the field led to a five-year contract worth an astounding $260 million after the 2022 season.

Career and 2023 Season Performance

In 2023, Jackson continued to impress, leading the Ravens to the top seed in the conference and clinching his second NFL MVP title. His achievements on the field are nothing short of remarkable, with records such as the most rushing yards by a quarterback on Monday Night Football and multiple seasons with over 1,000 rushing yards, solidifying his status as one of the league's top talents.

Why Consider a Trade?

While Lamar Jackson's contribution to the Ravens has been invaluable, there are compelling reasons why the team might consider trading him:

  • Contract Implications: Jackson's mammoth five-year contract places a significant financial burden on the Ravens, limiting their flexibility in building a well-rounded team.
  • Injury Concerns: Jackson's style of play as a dual-threat quarterback exposes him to a higher risk of injuries, as evidenced by his absence in the 2022 season due to an ankle injury.
  • Team Rebuilding: With the potential to secure valuable draft picks or players in exchange for Jackson, the Ravens could use a trade to bolster their roster and set the stage for a successful rebuilding phase.

Conclusion

Lamar Jackson's impact on the Baltimore Ravens and the NFL as a whole cannot be understated. His electrifying performances and remarkable journey to stardom have left an indelible mark on the sport. However, considering the team's financial and strategic considerations, a trade involving Jackson could be a viable option for the Ravens to ensure long-term success.

As the NFL landscape continues to evolve, the possibility of a trade involving a talent like Lamar Jackson is a thought-provoking prospect that could reshape the future of the league.

In conclusion, while the idea of trading a player of Jackson's caliber may seem unfathomable, the potential benefits for both the Ravens and Jackson himself cannot be overlooked. It's an intriguing proposition that could redefine the dynamics of the NFL and pave the way for new opportunities for all parties involved.
