Shayne Topp - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Single, Facts

Table of Biography

Shayne Topp is an American actor, famous for the roles in the comedy show ‘Smosh’, Matt Bradley in ‘The Goldbergs series, and Shayne Zabo in ‘The So Random!’ series. Shayne Topp has also played in TV series such as Sam & Cat, iCarly, Switched at Birth, and Henry Danger.

Early Life and Childhood

Shayne Topp is a 32-year-old actor who was born on 14th September 1991 in Los Angeles, California, USA. Shyne grew up in a family of pilots. Shyne Topp’s parents are Catherine and Robert Royal Topp. He also has two brothers named Brian Topp and Chrish Topp.

He almost drowned in a swimming pool when he was 18 months old. Fortunately, his parents saved him in the nick of time.

After that, his father started calling him ‘Shayne Bobber,’ as he was observed bobbing up and down in the pool before being comforted by his mother. He is the youngest of three sons in the family.

Likewise, his father was an army man who worked for the ‘US Air Force’ as a fighter pilot. Before him, his grandfather also worked as a fighter pilot. Thus, he was expected to be a pilot. At a very early age, he had a keen interest in acting.

Awards

Shyne won the Jury Prize award for ‘Outstanding Performance in the Narrative Competition’ in Dear Lemon Lima in the year 2009 at the Los Angeles Film Festivals.

He also got a nomination at the 2014 Young Hollywood Awards for the ‘Exciting New Face – Male’ for the same role in the same film he performed.

Personal Life

As of now, Shayne Topp is probably single. Shyne was in a relationship before he began his acting in the film Smosh Summer Games – Wild West. Their relationship ended exactly five hours before the shoot of the film. He is also a fan of Game of Thrones.

Professional Life

Shayne Topp made his acting debut with the series ‘Moonpie’ in the year 2006 when he just turned 15 years old. He played a small role in the ‘iCarly series in 2008. He appeared in films such as ‘The Violation’ and ‘Hi, Lillian’.

However, his first stint with acting took place when he was in fifth grade, and played a small role in the school production of ‘Willie Wonka and the Chocolate Factory.’

Later, he continued to act throughout his school years and remained excellent at “improv” comedy and impersonations. He started attending acting classes before plunging into professional acting.

2008-2014

After completing high school in 2008, he made an appearance in an episode of the popular series ‘iCarly’. Also, he played ‘Phillip Brownley,’ a small role, in an episode titled ‘Might Switch Schools.’

In 2009, he debuted the first major role of his acting career in the film ‘Dear Lemon Lima.’ The following film is based on the short film and featured him as ‘Philip Georgey.’

The film was released at the ‘Los Angeles Film Festival’ in 2009 and in theaters in 2011, and it got a warm response from both critics and audiences.

Likewise, his performance in the movie gained him an award for ‘Outstanding Performance’ in the narrative competition at the ‘Los Angeles Film Festival.’ In 2010, he was cast in the legal drama ‘Eli Stone’ and made an appearance in its pilot episode.

Though he was unable to find bigger roles, he resorted to doing smaller roles in several series during the early 2010s.

In 2011, he appeared in a small single episode in the series ‘Love Bites.’ Next year, he played the lead role of  ‘Corey,’ in the independent film ‘All Kids Count.’

In 2012, he made a guest appearance in the series ‘Fred: The Show.’ Later, he also appeared in the episode titled ‘Expired Cow.’ In the following year, he breaks through as one of the cast members of the famous ‘Disney’ sketch comedy show ‘So Random!’.

Moreover, Shayne Topp played the recurring role of ‘Shayne Zabo.’ The show hit success with audiences and critics, and his performance made the show worth watching.

It was one of his most popular appearances on TV. He turned to short films toward the end of 2012 and made appearances in leading roles in films such as ‘Hi, Lillian’ and ‘The Violation.’

2013-present

In the following two years, he acted in two more short films, ‘First’ and ‘Re-Writes.’ He made a single-episode guest appearance for ‘AwesomenessTV’ in 2013.

Also, in 2014, he performed minor single-episode roles in the series ‘Sam & Cat’ and ‘What’s Next for Sarah?’. In 2015, he played the role of ‘Taylor Halsted,’ another single-episode role, in the series ‘Switched at Birth.’

In 2015, Shayne Topp played a key role in an independent film titled ‘A Sort of Homecoming.’ It premiered at the ‘Omaha Film Festival’ but was met with heavy critical acclaim.

Later, he played the role of ‘Dylan Conti.’ Eventually, the film was released digitally in the US and received a good response from the audience. In 2015, he appeared in minor roles in series such as ‘Henry Danger’ and ‘Astrid Clover.’

In 2016, he made an appearance in the series Cell Outs.’ Notwithstanding his critically acclaimed performances, he continued ignored by Hollywood producers and ultimately decided to leave the industry for good, after failing to bag a good film role.

When he was about to leave Los Angeles for good, one of his colleagues from his acting classes urged him to audition for ‘Smosh,’ an American sketch-comedy ‘YouTube’ channel.

He appeared for the audition and got a chance to play several random characters. The channel has been the most-subscribed ‘YouTube’ channel at three separate times ever since it began posting videos.‘

Further, he worked with other cast members on the show and has garnered praise by impersonating actors such as Tom Cruise, Christopher Walken, Nicholas Cage, and Michael Caine.

Likewise, he also joined the cast of the series ‘The Goldbergs in 2017 and plays the recurring role of ‘Matt Bradley.’
