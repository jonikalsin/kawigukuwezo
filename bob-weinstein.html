Bob Weinstein Biography

Bob Weinstein was born on October 18, 1954, to a Jewish Ashkenazi family in Flushing, New York. He had a difficult upbringing because his family wasn’t financially secure. His mother, Miriam Weinstein, was a stay-at-home mom, while his father, Max Weinstein, was a diamond cutter. He and his older brother Harvey Weinstein were raised together in the Electchester neighborhood of New York City. Along with his older brother, he attended John Bowne High School, where he received his diploma.

Bob Weinstein Career

Bob Weinstein, his brother Harvey Weinstein, and their business partner Corky Burger founded the successful Buffalo-based production company known as Harvey & Corky Productions. Both of the brothers had a love of theater and cinema as children. Festivals and concerts were some of their first endeavors.

They launched an independent film production company called Miramax, named after their parents Miriam and Max, with the money they made from the same business. At first, Miramax organized musical events like Paul McCartney’s Rockshow, which was well-liked by the audience. Martin Lewis, a well-known producer of original films around that time, was a close collaborator for them. The Secret Policeman’s Other Ball, a movie that came out in May, was Miramax’s first big success.
